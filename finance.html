Financial overview

Cost structure, revenue logic, and illustrative scaling for OperO as a modular, institution-facing campus operations platform.

The model assumes institutional buyers (universities), not individual students: revenue combines one-time setup, annual subscription, and optional module fees—a pattern typical of early-stage B2B SaaS for large organisations.

Startup costs (one-time)

Capital required to reach a deployable product and first paying institutions.

  • Product & platform development: €60,000
  • Cloud & infrastructure setup: €12,000
  • APIs & integrations tooling: €3,000
  • Mobile distribution (Apple Developer): €99
  • Mobile distribution (Google Play): €25
  • Total: €75,124

Operating costs (run-rate)

Monthly spend to maintain, host, and support the live platform.

  • Maintenance & support: €5,000 / month
  • Cloud hosting & services: €1,000 / month
  • Indicative total: €6,000 / month

Revenue streams (per university)

Blended contract value for onboarding one institution onto the core platform plus optional modules.

  • Implementation & setup fee: €20,000
  • Annual platform subscription: €15,000
  • Additional modules (optional): €3,000
  • Illustrative total per institution: €38,000

Illustrative surplus (3 institutions)

Simple check: cumulative contract value versus one-time startup spend (before ongoing opex detail).

  • Revenue (3 × illustrative contract mix): €114,000
  • Less: startup costs (one-time): €75,124
  • Estimated surplus (pre-opex): €38,876
